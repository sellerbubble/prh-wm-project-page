# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary users are ML, robotics, representation learning, and world-model researchers who want to understand the NeurIPS paper faster than by reading the full manuscript. Secondary users are technically literate readers who need a clear project-page summary before deciding whether to read the PDF.

## Product Purpose

This static site explains the project-page version of "Platonic Representation Hypothesis on World Models." Success means a visitor can quickly understand the claim, setup, evidence, takeaways, scope, and paper links without the page feeling like a marketing landing page.

## Brand Personality

Clear, restrained, research-forward.

The site should feel like a careful academic exhibit: precise enough for reviewers, visually composed enough to make the argument easier to scan, and never louder than the evidence.

## Anti-references

Avoid AI-purple gradients, glassy SaaS hero sections, repeated identical cards, excessive animation, decorative visual noise, and marketing CTA language. Avoid visual treatments that make the paper feel like a product launch rather than a research project.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the argument easier than the paper, not bigger than the paper.
2. Preserve content and scientific claims exactly across visual variants.
3. Let figures carry evidence; do not bury them behind decoration.
4. Use restraint as a form of credibility.
5. Make each variant clearly distinguishable through layout, rhythm, and material, not through flashy effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target readable contrast, keyboard-friendly links and buttons, responsive desktop and mobile layouts, and minimal motion. Motion should be optional, subtle, and nonessential.
